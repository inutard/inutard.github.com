Projects

Ongoing:

  • I am currently continuing research with professor Chen Greif (UBC). We hope to extend the package we wrote in the summer to skew-symmetric matrices as well. A proposal of our goals can be found here.

2012:

  • Participated in the 2012 University Physics Contest. We were ranked Bronze! You can see the paper we wrote for the contest here. The question was to determine the optimal radius of a ping pong ball for maximum "fun".
  • Participated in the 2012 ACM ICPC PAC-NW Regionals. We placed 3rd out of around 110 teams. You can view the final results here. Unfortunately, Stanford took 1st and 2nd place. However, since UBC is one of the top two schools, we have qualified for the 2013 ACM ICPC World Finals! Hopefully I won't let anybody down!
  • Gave a short speech to all UBC Math students on the first day of school on summer research, the Putnam Contest, and the UBC Math Circle.
  • With funding by NSERC, I worked under professor Chen Greif (UBC) in the field of numerical linear algebra. Together we created a small but powerful package designed to give fast incomplete factorizations of symmetric indefinite matrices. Further reading on the package can be found here.
  • For UBC Students, here is a small chrome extension to automatically login into SSC and Vista. After installing this plugin, a small bird will appear in the top right corner of the chrome browser. Enter your login info and then try going to SSC or Vista! As of Nov. 11, 2012, you have to drag and drop the extension into the extensions page at chrome://extensions for this to work. This is due to Chromes new security restrictions.
  • Gave a short talk at the UBC Math Circle on cellular automata. The slides to the presentation can be found here.
  • Gave a short talk for the UBC CS490 Seminar on Number Theory. The slides to the presentation can be found here.
